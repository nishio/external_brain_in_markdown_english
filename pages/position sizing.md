Objective.
- Identify how much risk you are willing to take relative to your account funds.
- Protect funds in the event of losses and build positions to the extent that profits can be targeted.

Examples of Concepts
- Fixed risk method: A fixed percentage of the account, such as 1% or 2% of the total account, is set as the risk tolerance.
- Volatility method: Adjust position size according to market volatility
- Indicators such as ATR (Average True Range) may be utilized

Advantages/Disadvantages
- Benefit: Continuous risk management, not driven by emotions or impulses
- Disadvantage: If you are overly conservative, you may miss out on big opportunities.
