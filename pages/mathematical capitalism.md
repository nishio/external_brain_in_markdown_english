> Mathematical capitalism" has the meaning of "an economic system in which mathematics is the source of national wealth ([[capitalism]])" as well as "a way of thinking that emphasizes human resources in the sciences and mathematics as capital ([[actuarial capital]]). The country that builds a new management and social system suitable for "mathematical capitalism" in both of these senses will be the winner of the fourth industrial revolution.
[A report on a meeting for exchanging opinions on the activities of science and mathematics personnel in industry, "The Age of Mathematical Capitalism: The Power of Mathematics Will Change the World", has been compiled (METI/Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry) [https://www.meti.go.jp/press/2018/03/20190326005/](https://www.meti.go.jp/press/2018/03/20190326005/) 20190326005.html]

The "[[knowledge capital]] principle," which does not limit capital to monetary items but considers knowledge to be capital as well, has been well known for some time, but I understood "mathematical capitalism" to be a more explicit statement that "human resources possessing mathematical knowledge are capital.
