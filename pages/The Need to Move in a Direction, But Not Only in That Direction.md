title: "The Need to Move in a Direction, But Not Only in That Direction"  
---

To reach the [[ideal]], it is necessary to move in the direction of x, but if you move only in the direction of x, you will fall off a cliff.  
![image](https://gyazo.com/ca7be5ff97369dabf2629741660d2b21/thumb/1000)

> [@AntiBayesian](https://twitter.com/AntiBayesian/status/1376851751395946500?s=20&t=ZPeeYQATmOFApfDvmM6DJw): There was an organization created with the beautiful [[vision]] of "creating an [[organization]] where it is easy to speak up."  
> While the capable people quietly solved problems, the less capable ones stopped to discuss how the organization was not perfect and continued to debate this issue, leading to its collapse.  
> A vision is not good just because it is practiced.

> [@nishio](https://twitter.com/nishio/status/1377013011370500098?s=20&t=Dp_rk6GwcatdbjJWWzMpgA): Why didn't they say, "We can't just stop and debate"?  
> [@nishio](https://twitter.com/nishio/status/1377013500858396673?s=20&t=Dp_rk6GwcatdbjJWWzMpgA): Well, there is a sense that a single axis can be both good and bad, and it is only by combining several axes that you can finally move in a good direction.
