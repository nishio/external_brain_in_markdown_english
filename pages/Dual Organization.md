- An example of [[Blurring of Organizational Boundaries]]
- In an [[Open Source Community]], participation in mailing lists is free, but becoming someone who actually changes the source code (a person who commits changes to the repository = [[Committer]]) requires special authorization ([[Selection]]).
    - Committers have opportunities for in-depth discussions on the overall direction of the project
    - Recruitment into the internal community is based on activities in the external community
