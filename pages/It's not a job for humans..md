---

Phrases that software engineers inadvertently use and are interpreted by people of a different culture as strong occupational discrimination and don't

To put it more moderately, it's like, "I think that can be handled more efficiently with a computer."

It's typical to use "human" as the subject "[[the subject is large]]."
- Really means "I don't want to do that job, I want the computer to do it."
- It is [[overgeneralization]] to extend it to different people because each of these "selves" has a different weighting of time, motivation, and money.
    - There are many people who would like to have AI do a job if they can pay 10 yen as the call cost of AI, but when the cost becomes 100 yen, 1,000 yen, or 10,000 yen, many people give up the option of having AI do the job.
        - In that case, sometimes it's "I'll do it myself" and sometimes it's "I won't do it."
        - Those who "don't do it" aren't originally so eager to make it happen.
