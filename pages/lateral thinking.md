> Lateral thinking is a method of generating ideas without being bound by established theories or concepts in order to solve problems. [It was first proposed by Edward Debono around 1967.
>  De Bono refers to conventional logical and analytical thinking as Vertical thinking, which is effective for deepening logic, but difficult to generate novel ideas. In contrast, horizontal thinking is a method that generates intuitive ideas by looking at things from a variety of perspectives.
