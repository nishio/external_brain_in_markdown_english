- Instead of a pre-designed classification tree, a knowledge network is developed by adding links (=tags) that spontaneously arise from the memos.

Core Principles (excerpts)
- Top-down classification is minimal: assumption that structure changes with additional knowledge. Fixed trees lead to "dead text". Store in a network.
- Linking is the tag: **page link** rather than #hash, and use line links to establish a concrete relationship. Do not break references just by copying them.
- Visualize moments of convergence: lines connecting different groups = convergent thinking (critical backlink). This is the point of progress of knowledge.
- Life cycle of ideas:
    - Undeveloped (hypothetical sprout) ‚Üí Stable (reappearance of isomorphism) ‚Üí Inclusive (combines with other elements and becomes higher order)/Falls (dismissed due to contradiction). Record and turn.

Operational flow (divergence ‚Üí convergence in the shortest distance)
1. divergence (10 minutes)
    - Drop a small specific memo (1 memo = 1 claim/1 fact).
    - If in doubt, don't force tagging = put it underdeveloped.
2. light convergence (10 minutes)
    - Add at least two links to each memo (e.g., related concepts/ counterexamples).
    - Use line links to clarify "which line and which line resonated" (aiming for critical backlinks).
3. making a nameplate (10 minutes)
    - Attach headings and one-sentence summaries to adjacent pages (the "faceplates" of the KJ method).
    - Assign a "state tag" on the spot: üçºUndeveloped/üß±Stable/üï∏Inclusive/üóëFallen.
Point: Write first, then connect later, then summarize, in this order, and then write a clean copy (one-dimensional book) at the end.

Linking & Tagging Conventions (Mini-Conventions)
- Noun for page name (short and unambiguous)/synonym puts a thin page for redirects.
- Line link first: Copying and pasting quotations is convenient, but breaks references. Link to the original line whenever possible.
- A minimum set of related terms (optional): `is-a / part-of / supports / refutes / example-of` may be written at the beginning of each line (over-labeling is x).

Measure and review (lightweight KPIs)
- Isolation rate: Isolated pages divided by total pages (the lower the better).
- Average number of backlinks: a measure of convergence (the higher the better).
- Critical ratio: percentage of rows with multiple backlinks (the "bud" of convergence).
- Number of inclusive events: Number of newly established links between different clusters.
In both cases, it is sufficient to use Scrapbox's list of links for manual measurement ‚Üí count only monthly.

anti-pattern
- Make a huge category table ahead of time/struggle with classification as you write.
- Reference cutoff by copying and pasting (erasing evidence of convergence).
- Premature clearing (tree fixing) kills the network.
